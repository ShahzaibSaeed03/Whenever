What Is Copyright?

Copyright is a legal right that protects the original works of creators. When you create something unique, like a story, a song, or a design, copyright grants you the exclusive right to use, distribute, and benefit from your work. It ensures that your creativity and effort are recognized and safeguarded from unauthorized use.

Where Does Copyright Apply?

Copyright laws exist in nearly every country worldwide. International agreements such as the Berne Convention extend protection across borders in more than 180 countries, including the United States, the European Union, and the United Kingdom.

What Can Copyright Protect?

1. Literary Works

Novels, short stories, poems, essays, scripts, academic papers, software code.

2. Musical Works

Songs, instrumental compositions, sheet music, arrangements.

3. Dramatic Works

Plays, scripts, choreography.

4. Visual Artworks

Paintings, drawings, photography, sculptures, architecture.

5. Audiovisual Works

Movies, series, documentaries, animations.

6. Sound Recordings

Music recordings, audiobooks, podcasts.

7. Broadcasts

Television, radio, online streaming.

8. Industrial & Commercial Designs

Logos, POD designs, packaging.

9. Digital & Multimedia Works

Websites, video games, UI design, NFTs.

10. Written Communications

Letters, diaries, speeches.

11. Applied Art

Furniture, fashion, jewelry.

12. Compilations & Derivatives

Databases, translations, adaptations.

13. AI-Generated Works

AI text, images, music (jurisdiction dependent).

14. Miscellaneous

Maps, typography, emblems.

Important Notes

  • Originality is Key: work must be original.
  • Fixed Medium: work must be recorded or saved.
  • Non-Copyrightable: facts and ideas are not protected.
